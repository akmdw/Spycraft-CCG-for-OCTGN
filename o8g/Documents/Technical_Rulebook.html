1 Deck Rules

Decks must consist of:

4 leaders, one of each level 1­4 from the same faction

If the level 3 and 4 leaders have Master or Henchman traits, then both the named Master and Henchman must be used

At least 46 non­leader cards

No more than 3 copies of any named card, or 1 copy of any card with the Unique trait

The deck's faction is determined by the player's level 1 leader.

When an effect 'searches' a deck for another card, if there is a restriction on the type of card that may be retrieved, then the player must reveal that card to his opponents. After an effect searches a deck, the player must then shuffle the deck.

When a deck is exhausted of cards, the discard pile for the deck is reshuffled. If there are cards still to be drawn, they are then drawn from the newly shuffled deck. If after the discard pile is reshuffled, there are still not enough cards to fulfill the requirements of the effect, then the game continues after attempting and failing to draw/examine the cards, due to the infinite loop breaking rule.

2 Game State

The following states are globally maintained by the game, henceforth the 'game state':

The location of a card (at headquarters, a mission, attached to another card...)

The ability of a card to be activated

The controller of a card

The orientation of a card (face up, face down, rotated...)

If the state of the game reaches an infinite loop, then the loop proceeds for one iteration and then stops. For example, if there is an effect which reads “After an action is canceled, wound the acting agent” and a second effect which reads “Before an agent is wounded, cancel the action” and an agent is wounded, then the game would first apply the second effect canceling the wounding action, then it would apply the first effect wounding the acting agent. It would not then continue to try and cancel the action again with the second effect.

Cards in play 'forget' all status effects when they are either activated or deactivated. All tokens are removed at either change of state. Cards attached to an agent that is activated or deactivated are unaffected.

3 Actions

There are three types of action:

Intel actions may be performed during the Intel phase, once per turn per printed instance.

Mission actions may be performed during the Mission phase, once per mission per printed instance.

Reacts are performed in response to trigger events described on the card, once per turn per printed instance. They must be performed in the window which corresponds to their trigger text.

Actions are performed by an agent, unless they are 'enforced actions'. The agent performing the action is 'this agent' for the current action and 'the acting agent' for reacts to the current action.

'Perform' lasts for the entire duration of the action, from declaration through to resolution.

3.1 Generic Action Timing

All actions have the following basic timing, with all of the react windows included. The main phases of the action are in bold.

1.Reacts before player declares if they will take an action

2.Active player declares if they will pass or take an action.

3.Reacts after player declares if they will take an action

4.Reacts before declaration of action

5.Active player declares the action that will be performed and the acting agent.

6.Reacts after declaration of action

7.Reacts before targeting

8.Active player declares the targets (if any) for the action, in order of the active player's choosing

9.(React windows to individual cards being targeted, as they are targeted)

10.Reacts after targeting

11.Reacts before costs are paid

12.Costs for the action are paid, in the order of the active player's choosing

13.(React windows to individual costs, as they are paid)

14.Reacts after costs are paid

15.Reacts before effects are produced

16.Effects of the action are produced, in the order they appear in the action text

17.(React windows to individual effects, as they are produced)

18.Reacts after the action has been resolved

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

3.2 Declaration of Pass or Action

The active player declares whether they will take an action or pass. If they pass, then the next player becomes the active player and the timing starts again. If they choose to take an action, the timing continues.

3.3 Declaration of Action and Agent

The active player declares which active agent will perform the action and the action that they will perform. If the action is from an action card, it is discarded. Otherwise the action may come from the agent's text box, an active gear attached to the agent, or an action given to the agent by the rulebook or another card.

Actions given by a card or the rulebook are considered to be printed on the agent for the purposes of declaration, and hence are subject to usage limits. If an action given by the rulebook or a trait is removed or modified, then the rulebook or trait immediately creates a new copy of the action.

Illegal actions may not be declared. Actions which become illegal after declaration fail as soon as they become illegal.

3.4 Declaration of Targets

The active player declares in the order of their choosing the targets, if any, of the action. Any card which is affected by the effect of the action is targeted by the action at this stage, unless the action text specifically says that it targets during the effects phase, or there is a choice where it is possible that no agent is targeted.

3.4.1 Targeting Face Down Cards

Cards that are face down but not inactive may be targeted without restriction (e.g. Missions) Inactive cards may not be targeted unless the action specifically allows inactive cards to be targeted. Inactive agents at missions may be targeted.

If an inactive card is targeted by an action or effect that has any effect other than to examine it, reveal it or, if the card is controlled by the active player, change its game state, then it is force activated. See Enforced Actions 5.4

Additionally, if the effect is globally targeting and only changes a numeric value for the card (skill, bravado, expense, cover) then inactive cards are not force activated, nor affected.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

3.4.2 Redirection

Some Reacts will allow targeting to be changed after it is declared. First the redirecting action targets the card which will be the new target for the original action. Then the effect is to redirect the targeting of the original action from the original target to that card.

Redirection must be to a target which was legal for the original action, from the original acting player's perspective.

3.4.3 Loss of Targets

Targets of an action may be lost before the action's effects are processed, typically due to reacts to targeting.

If at the end of the targeting phase, all of the targets of an action are lost, then the action becomes illegal and fails.

If some, but not all, of the targets of an action are lost and the targets are not interdependent, then the remaining targets are affected as normal. If the targets that remain depend on targets that have been lost, then the action becomes illegal.

3.5 Costs

Costs will always involve a skill check, but actions may have one or more additional costs.

The template for costs of Intel and Mission actions:

ActionType Skill TN : AdditionalCosts :

ActionType is required and one of Intel, Mission or React.

Skill is required and one of Charisma, Combat, Transport or Craft.

TN is short for Target Number, and optional. If not present it defaults to 0. It may also be X, where the TN is variable and defined later in the text of the action.

Additional costs may be present between two sets of colons.

The template for costs of React actions:

ActionType Skill TN : Trigger , AdditionalCosts :

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

The two colons are required for Reacts, and there must be a Trigger section before any additional costs, specifying when the react is valid to be performed.

A single card may be discarded from your hand as 'Bravado'. You may not discard for bravado if you would succeed without it. Discarding for bravado is not an action or a 'discard'.

To succeed, the agent must have a skill + bravado greater than or equal to the TN.

You do not have to attempt to meet any cost that is not automatically met. For example, you cannot choose to not meet a TN that the acting agent does not have to discard for bravado to meet, but you can choose to not pay the cost of moving an agent from a mission to headquarters. If all costs are met, then the effects are generated, otherwise the action fails (and is not successfully resolved) and no effects are generated.

3.6 Effects

Once all costs are paid, then the effects of the action are generated. Any effect which modifies the TN of the action at this stage makes no difference.

The duration of any effect, unless otherwise specified, is until the end of the turn. The maximum duration of any effect on a card is until the affected card leaves play.

The maximum duration for any non game state effect on a card is until the affected card leaves play or is deactivated. Also known as 'permanent'.

The duration for Wounded and Exposed is permanent, unless otherwise specified. After all effects have happened, the action is 'resolved'.

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

4 Actions Given By Rulebook

4.1 Reacts

4.1.1 Activate an Agent

Your active leader may perform:

React Craft: Before or after any trigger: Activate an inactive target agent that you control. This action may be performed any number of times per turn. This action may be performed from headquarters. This action may not be canceled or redirected.

This action has an additional cost of the leader's printed expense (+ optional bravado) must be greater than or equal to the expense of the agent. If the player does not meet all of the costs of the action, the agent is discarded.

If the agent to be activated comes from a different, non Freelancer, faction to the leader, then the agent's expense is double for the purposes of activation, before any other modifiers are applied. This is called the loyalty penalty.

If the agent to be activated comes from a different, non Freelancer faction to the leader, and there is an active enemy leader of that faction, then that agent cannot be activated.

4.1.2 Activate a Gear

All of your active agents may perform:

React Craft: Before or after any trigger: Activate an inactive target gear attached to this agent. This action may be performed any number of times per turn. This action may be performed from headquarters. This action may not be canceled or redirected.

This action has an additional cost of the agents current expense (+ optional bravado) must be greater than or equal to the expense of the gear. If the player does not meet all of the costs of the action, the gear is discarded.

If an agent activates or attaches a gear that shares a name or trait other than 'Unique' or 'Gadget', the rulebook enforces the discard of one of the gear (see Enforced Actions 5.9)

4.1.3 Activate a Leader

Your active leader may perform:

React Craft: Before or after any trigger: Activate an inactive leader that you control. This action may be performed any number of times per turn. This action may be performed from headquarters. This action may not be canceled or redirected.

5 Enforced Actions

The rulebook takes actions at certain times. These actions are enforced and players have no choice but to process them as soon as the condition that triggers them happens. They may be considered to be highest priority reacts with no acting agent or player, and are expressed as such below. There are also enforced actions due to traits on cards. Enforced actions are processed with the same timing rules as normal actions, apart from the declaration to pass or take an action.

5.1 Leader Forced Activation

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After a player's active leader is removed from the game: Activate the player's lowest level inactive leader without cost.

5.2 Leader Demotion

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After any player's leader is activated: Permanently remove the leader trait from the player's lower­level active leader.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

5.3 Redundant Leader Removal

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: Before or after any trigger: Remove from the game any inactive leader card which is owned by a player controlling a higher level active leader.

5.4 Agent Forced Activation

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After an inactive agent is targeted by an action that would force activation: Activate the targeted agent.

5.5 Agent Forced Retirement (Team Size)

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After an agent is activated or changes controller: If the agent's controller controls more active agents than the current maximum team size, the agent's controller must target an active non­leader agent. Retire the target. This action may not be canceled or redirected.

5.6 Agent Forced Retirement (Mission Team Size)

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After an agent is moved into a mission team: If the agent's mission team has more agents than the current maximum mission team size, the mission team's controller must target a non­leader agent at the mission. Retire the target. This action may not be canceled or redirected.

5.7 Agent Forced Movement to Headquarters

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After an agent is assigned to a mission: If the agent's mission team has more agents that the current maximum mission team size, the agent's controller must target one of their agents assigned to the mission. Move the target to headquarters.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

5.8 Agent Retirement due to Wounds

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After any trigger: If the agent has two or more wounds, retire the agent.

While an agent has one or more wounds, he has a penalty of ­2 to all of his skills.

5.9 Gear Retirement

The rulebook must perform:

Enforced React: After a gear is activated or attached to an agent: If the gear shares the same name or a trait other than 'Gadget' or 'Unique' with another gear attached to the same agent, retire the other (original) gear.

6 Game Timing and Configuration

6.1 Pre­Game Setup

There are a number of steps to take before the beginning of the game:

1.One player must provide a current Mission Deck

2.All players put their leaders into play as inactive

3.All players shuffle their agency decks

4.All players activate their level 1 leader

5.All players draw 7 cards from their agency deck

6.Any player may reshuffle their hand into their deck and draw 6 cards

7.One player deals the mission queue

6.1.1 The Mission Queue

The mission queue is five mission cards from the mission deck. If there are fewer than five cards in the queue other than during a mission phase, then move each mission in order to the earliest empty slot and fill the slots at the end with missions from the top of the deck. If either of the first two missions are ever face down, turn it face up.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

6.2 Game Turn Timing

The game consists of sequential turns. Each turn consists of the following phases:

1.The turn starts, and the priority player is determined.

2.Reacts after the start of the turn

3.Reacts before the Intel phase begins

4.The Intel Phase, including any number of subsidiary Mission Phases

5.Reacts after the Intel phase ends

6.Reacts before the Debrief phase begins

7.The Debrief phase

8.Reacts after the Debrief phase ends

9.Reacts before the end of the turn

10.The turn ends

6.3 The Priority Player

The priority player is determined at the beginning of the turn. It is the player who controls the active agents with the highest combined craft skill. If there is a tie, the priority player is determined randomly from those tied. Once the priority player is determined, play moves in a clockwise direction. The priority player has the first opportunity to perform reacts to any trigger.

During a mission, the priority player changes to the player who controls the active agents with the highest craft assigned to the mission. See the Mission Phase timing below.

The player who currently has the opportunity to play or pass is the Active player.

6.4 Intel Phase Timing

After the Intel phase begins, the priority player has the first choice to act or pass. Play continues clockwise until all players pass consecutively on the opportunity to play an Intel action, at which point play progresses to the Debrief phase.

Mission phases are within the Intel phase. After each mission phase ends, return to the Intel phase.

6.5.1Mission Planning

The attacking player declares which mission will be run, and the player's leader targets the mission. Only missions in the queue may be targeted. Once a mission has been targeted, it will be run even if its location should change.

If the mission is face down, it is then turned face up.

The attacker then declares which active or inactive agents will be assigned to the mission. The maximum mission team size is 5 agents and the player's active leader. The attacker must assign at least one agent, if possible.

6.5.2 Identify the Enemy

Beginning with the player to the attacker's left, each other player has the option to either defend the mission or pass. If all other players have passed, the last player is the defender.

The defender then declares his mission team, as above except that the minimum number of agents is zero. The defender does not target the mission.

After the defender has assigned his mission team, the priority player is determined for the mission, as per section 6.3.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

6.5.3 Engage the Enemy

Beginning with the new priority player, the attacker and defender may take mission actions until both players pass consecutively on the opportunity to take a mission action. Any player may take react actions, pending the following rules.

For this phase, agents and leaders not on a mission team may not perform actions, unless:

●the action will move the acting agent into the mission

●the action is a react to an action targeting the agent

●the action is the 'Activate an Agent' react performed by your leader

●the action is specifically permitted by the action's text.

6.5.4 Mission Goals

If the Attacker's agents' total skill on his mission team is greater than or equal to the mission objective plus the defender's agents' total skill on their mission team, he wins the mission. Otherwise, the Defender wins the mission.

6.5.5 Mission Debrief

The player that won the mission, adds the victory point value of the mission to their total. The mission is placed in the winning players headquarters. Completed missions are neither active nor inactive and may not be targeted by actions, unless the action specifically allows it.

The defender's mission team returns to headquarters. This is not considered movement.

If the Attacker won the mission, he may declare that he is extending the mission phase. His leader targets the next, adjacent mission in the queue. The first mission in the queue is the next, adjacent mission to the last mission, or any mission not in the queue. Turn the mission face up, if it is face down. The attacker's current mission team are then assigned to the mission.

The mission's objective is doubled for each extension the attacker has declared that turn.

If the Defender won the mission, all agents are returned to headquarters and the mission phase is over.

After the mission phase ends, the mission queue is refilled and play resumes in the Intel Phase with the player to the left of the Attacker. The priority player returns to being the player who was the priority player for the Intel Phase.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

6.6 Debrief Phase

At the end of the Debrief phase, all players draw five cards and then discards to their maximum hand size. The default maximum hand size is seven.

Timing for the Debrief Phase:

1.Reacts before the Debrief Phase

2.Players draw five cards

3.Reacts

4.Players discard down to maximum hand size

5.Reacts

6.End of Debrief phase

7 Traits, Skills and Numeric Statistics

Traits are constantly active. If the trait has a condition that must be fulfilled and an effect to generate when that trigger is met, it is an enforced action performed by the rulebook, not by the card the trait is on.

The Base Skill of an agent is equal to the skill value printed on the agent's card. Base skills may change, whereas obviously the printed value cannot change.

Temporary Effects are the result of an action and expire at a given time.

Ongoing Effects come from traits or attached gear. These persist while the trait exists or the gear is attached, and hence do not have a definite end time. Wounds are, thus, ongoing effects.

To calculate the current skill , cover or other statistic of a card:

1.Add together the bonuses, penalties or other effects from temporary effects in the order in which the effects were generated

2.Apply any ongoing bonuses, penalties or effects from ongoing, non­mission cards, in any order the controller of the agent desires

3.Apply ongoing bonuses, penalties or effects from ongoing effects from mission cards.

If the final value is below zero, then the final value is zero.

Unofficial Spycraft Technical Rules

(version 0.3, 2005­09­09)

 

 

8 Solo Ops

Solo ops are action cards with a particular card format. At the top of the text box is a line of bold text, which gives the solo op description. Below that may be the mission text for the solo op. Below that is one or more action descriptions. These actions only become available for use if the Attacker wins the solo op and the completed solo op is face up in his headquarters.

Solo ops do not have a mission type and cannot be given a mission type.

The mission text will give a condition or set of conditions that apply during the solo op.

Any agent controlled by the winning player can perform actions on solo ops. Each action, including mission actions, can only be used once per turn.

If the 'Run a Solo Op' action is successful, the revealed solo op counts as a mission card until a player wins the solo op Mission Phase. The Solo Op mission phase behaves like a normal mission phase, with the following exceptions:

A player may assign a maximum of one agent to a solo op and the maximum mission team size for a solo op is one agent.

Leaders cannot assign or move into solo ops.

An attacking agent that has won a solo op cannot be assigned or moved into another solo op during the same turn.

The cover for a solo op is the cover of the first mission in the mission queue.

It is impossible to extend from a solo op, either to a mission in the mission queue or to another solo op.

Determine the winning mission team as you would with any normal mission. The winning agent on a solo op is the agent that is present on the winning mission team after all players pass consecutively. If there is no agent on the winning mission team, the last agent to be on the winning mission team is the winning agent. Otherwise, there is no winning agent. A solo op that has been won is referred to as a completed solo op.

The player who wins the solo op gains 1 Victory Point, which cannot have its value modified in any way. If the Attacker wins, he places the completed solo op, face­up, in his headquarters.

If the Defender wins, the solo op is placed in its owner's discard pile and the Defender draws a card.

Completed solo ops that are placed face­up in a player's headquarters are neither active nor inactive and cannot be targeted, except by cards that specifically target completed solo ops.

Completed solo ops are not discarded for being face up, even though they are action cards. A player cannot have more than one completed solo op with any given name in his headquarters at any one time. If a player wins a solo op that shares a name with a completed solo op already in his headquarters, the old solo op is discarded and the newly won solo op is placed in headquarters.

A player can play Intel actions as normal after running a solo op and may run a normal mission in the same turn.

9 Glossary of Terms

Action: Actions are printed on action cards, gear and agents. There are three types of action – Intel,

Mission and React.

Acting agent: The agent performing the action which created the event referenced in the trigger of a react.

Activate/Activation: Activation is React Craft action which changes the game state of a card from inactive to active. Your leader activates agents and other leaders; gear is activated by the agent it is attached to.

Attach: When a card is played under another card, as when a card is played as gear under an agent. Previously, the verb 'equip' was also used.

Attacker: The player that declared the action to run a mission is the attacker. The attacker's team is the attacking team. All agents on the attacking team are attacking agents.

Base skill: Agents have four base skills – Charisma, Combat, Transport and Craft. Bonus: When an effect increases a number.

Bravado: The number in the bottom right hand corner of a card. Used to help meet target numbers and meet the expense when cards activate.

Brief: An action taken by your current leader to add activation tokens to your inactive leaders. Chase: One of the three types of Mission.

Controller: The player who puts a card into play is that card's controller, unless a card or rule states otherwise. Only a card's controller may use the card's abilities.

Deactivate: To return a card to inactive status (face down). Active leaders cannot be deactivated.

Debrief Phase: At the end of each turn, a phase in which players draw cards and discard to their maximum hand size.

Declare: Agents declare actions as the first part of performing it. Defender: The player that controls the mission team opposing the Attacker. Discard: To put a card into the discard pile.

Draw: To move a card from the agency deck to your hand. Enemy agent: An agent controlled by another player. Examine: To look at a face down card without activating it.

Expense: The number in the top right hand corner of agent and gear cards.

Exposed: A permanent trait given to agents when specified during the effects of actions. By itself the exposed trait does nothing.

Extending: After winning a mission, the Attacker may continue to the next mission in the queue. The difficulty of that mission is doubled and the Mission Phase continues.

Face up: A card whose art and text can be readily seen by all players. Active cards and the first two missions in the Mission Queue are face­up.

Face Down: A card that is not face up. Inactive cards are face down.

Factions: The Banshee Net, the Bloodvine Syndicate, the Franchise, the Krypt, the Nine Tiger Dynasty and the Shadow Patriots.

Fight: One of the three types of Mission

Forced activation: When you do not have a choice about the activation of an agent. Such a when an inactive agent on a mission team is targeted by an action.

Inactive: A face down non­mission card. Inactive cards have not been paid for.

Intel Phase: One of the three phases, players take it in turn playing Intel actions during this phase. Intrigue: One of the three types of Mission.

Loyalty penalty: Agents from factions other than your leader’s have double cost and cannot be activated if they are of the same faction as your opponents leaders.

Melee action: The actions text will tell you if the action is a melee action. An action that is also a melee action does not have any extra effects.

Mission: The cards in the mission deck and mission queue also a short­hand definition for the mission phase.

Mission card: One of the twenty four cards that make up the mission deck.

Mission objective: This is listed on a mission card as a skill and an Objective Number with a plus (“+”) symbol in front of it. The Attacker completes the mission objective and wins the mission if the total of the listed skill among active agents on the attacking mission team is equal to or greater than the Objective Number plus the total of the listed skill among all active agents on the defending mission team

Mission Phase: One of three phases of the game. Players may play mission actions during this phase.

Mission Team: A group of agents (including possibly a leader) that is on a mission as the attackers or defenders.

Mission type: There are three types of missions – intrigue missions, fight missions, and chase missions.

Move: When a card changes location. This is not considered switching locations. Previously 'send/sent' was used when referring to being moved to or from a mission.

Objective number: Listed on a mission card after the Objective skill, as a number with a plus (“+”) symbol in front of it. It helps make up a Mission Objective.

Objective Skill: Listed on a mission card before the Objective Number as a [skill – Charisma, Combat, Intrigue, Craft]. It helps make up a Mission Objective.

Opposing: An agent in the enemy mission team opposes all agents in your mission team.

Owner: The player whose deck a card was in when the game began, regardless of whether it is in play or who currently controls it. Cards and tokens created during the game are owned by their creator.

Penalty: When an effect decreases a number. Perform: Agents perform actions.

Permanent: Equivalent to ‘until the card leaves play or is deactivated.’

React: An action performed outside of the normal timing sequence in response to a trigger. All Reacts have a specific trigger listed in the text. Reacts do not count against your turn to perform Intel and Mission actions.

Remove from the game: Removed from play and cannot be returned without a specific card effect. A card “removed from the game” does not go to the discard pile; set it aside in a new, separate pile.

Retired: Removed from play and placed in the discard pile. Reveal: Show all players the card.

Search: Look through your deck or discard pile, usually for a specific type of card. Solo Op: A type of action card. Played with their own special rules.

Starting Player: The player with the highest total craft at the beginning of the Intel or mission phase is the starting player. This player has the first opportunity to pass or take an action during said phase. Also known as the Priority player.

Switch: When two cards change location with each other. This is not considered moving. Target(s): The card or cards that an action will affect.

Target Number (TN): The minimum skill rating required to perform an action successfully. Team: A player's agents at a mission.

“This agent”: The agent performing the current action.

Token: Physical objects used to record a piece of information. Some actions or abilities may create tokens.

Trait: A descriptive word in bold type found on many cards in the game. A card with a trait is a “[Trait] card”. For example, an agent with the Soldier trait is a “Soldier card”.

Trigger: The event that a react responds to.

Unique: You may only have one copy of a unique card in your deck.

Victory Points: The scoring system used in the game. You win as soon as you reach 20 victory points. Victory points are earned mainly by winning missions, but there are other methods of gaining them.

Wounded: Agents with a wound have –2 to all skills. A wounded agent that takes a second wound is retired.
