SPYCRAFT CCG REFERENCE SHEET

INTEL PHASE

1.Priority is determined (reacts may be declared before and after).

2.Declare any of the following in any order:

-Pass on Intel (once all players pass in a row, the Intel Phase Ends).

-Brief a leader (once per turn).

-Discard first mission in queue if that mission was the first mission in the queue at the start of the turn (thus one per turn total).

-Recruit an Agent (any number of times per turn).

-Requisition a Gear. (any number of times per turn).

-Snoop a face-down card: Craft 4 TN (once per acting agent).

-Intel action on a card in play (any number of times per turn, but

each instance of each action only once per turn).

-Intel action card from your hand (any number of times per turn).

-Run a Solo-Op A Mission Phase begins with the Solo-Op being the mission targeted. (any number of times per turn).

-Run a Mission A Mission Phase begins. (once per turn with a maximum number of times per turn of 1 less then the number or players who started the turn).

MISSION PHASE

1 Mission Planning step

a.Attacker targets a mission to run.

b.Attacker assigns mission team (minimum one agent). 2 Identify the Enemy step

a.In priority order, other players decide if they will defend or pass. The last player in order must defend, but need not send any agents.

b.Defender assigns mission team.

c.Priority is determined.

3 Engage the Enemy step

●Action that occur “during a mission” are played in this step.

●“Opposing” means “enemy agents at the same mission as the acting agent”.

●Durring this step, agents and leaders who are not on a mission team may NOT perform actions, UNLESS:

The action is the 'Activate an Agent' action.

The action will move the acting agent into the mission. The action is a react to an action targeting the agent.

The action is specifically permitted by the action's text.

Agents may declare any of the following in any order:

-Pass on mission action (once all players pass in a row, this step ends and the Mission proceds to step 4).

-Retreat (once per mission per Leader)

-Baffle (once per acting agent and only at a Intrigue)

-Evade (once per acting agent and only at a Chase)

-Shoot (once per acting agent and only at a Fight)

-Mission action on a card in play (any number of times per turn, but each instance of each action only once per turn).

-Mission action card from your hand.

4 Mission Goals step Winner is determined.

5 Mission Debrief step

a.Victory Points are added.

b.Defenders return to HQ.

c.Attack decides to extend or not.

d.If no extension, attackers return to HQ.

e.Mission Phase ends.

INTEL PHASE RESUMES

●Priority is the same as the first Intel Phase this turn.

●Players that ran a non-Solo-Op mission cannot perform any Intel actions after they ran and thus must Pass during this phase. They CAN still perform reacts.

DEBRIEF PHASE

1.Reorder stacks.

2.Each player draws five cards.

-Reacts (you MAY discard cards for bravado to pay for agents / gear before you discard down to your max hand-size.)

3.Each player discards down to seven cards.

4.End of the Debrief Phase (end of turn effects end).

ACTION TIMING

-React “Before pass or declare action”

1. Declare if pass or declare action.

-React “After pass or act

-React “Before declare / perform”

2. Declare action. (the agent starts “performing” the action)

-React “After declare”

-React “Before target”

3. Declare targets.

-React “After target”

-React “Before costs”

4. Pay costs.

-React “After costs”

-React “Before effect”

5. Resolve effects.

-React “After effect”

If and only if Step 5 occurs and after its react windows close, the action is “resolved”.

6. The action stops “resolving”.

- React “After resolve”

Once the chain is ended (due to cancellation, failure of payment, or resolving), there is another react window:

7. The agent stops “performing” the action.

-React “After perform”

Note, that an agent need NOT successfully resolve an action for there to be reacts to the agent “performing” the action.

TARGETING

-Inactive cards (face down non-missions) may not be targeted unless the effect specifically allows inactive cards to be targeted.

-Inactive agents AT missions MAY be targeted.

-If an inactive card is targeted by an action or effect that has any effect other than to examine it, reveal it or, changes its Game State, then it is force activated.

GAME STATE

The following states are globally maintained by the game, henceforth the 'game state':

●The controller of a card.

●The location of a card (at headquarters, a mission, attached to another card...).

●The orientation of a card (face up, face down, rotated...)

●The ability of a card to be activated.

MAXIMUM NUMBER OF AGENTS

-Maximum number or active (face up agents) per player side is 6 (Leader + 5 others).

-Maximum number or agents per player side that can be at a mission is 6 (Leader + 5 others).

-These two groups of six need not be the same six agents. For example, a player can have 3 face up agents at HQ while his Leader and 2 other face up agents are at a mission along with 3 face down agents at the mission.

-The maximum number of agents that can be at a Solo-Op per player side is one non-Leader. Leaders cannot ever be at a Solo-Op.

-If a player has too many agents active at one time, then that player must retire one of his/hers active agents at a time until there are no longer too many active agents.

-If a player has too many agents on a mission team, that player must retire one of his/hers agents (active or not) on the mission team until there are no longer too many agents on the mission team.
